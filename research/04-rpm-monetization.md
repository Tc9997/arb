# RPM and Monetization Research — Ad Revenue by Niche Vertical

**Bead:** arb-bo9
**Date:** 2026-03-23
**Purpose:** Evaluate display ad RPMs across niche verticals to inform programmatic SEO niche selection. Compare ad networks (Mediavine, Raptive, Ezoic), document seasonal RPM patterns, and map RPM expectations to the 13 candidate niches.

---

## Methodology

Data sourced from:
- Published ad network benchmarks (Mediavine, Raptive/AdThrive, Ezoic Niche Index)
- Blogger income reports (2024–2025)
- NeworMedia CPM calculator benchmarks
- Industry analyses from Setupad, MonetizeMore, Freestar
- Forum discussions (BlackHatWorld, niche site communities)

RPM = Revenue Per Mille (per 1,000 pageviews). EPMV = Earnings Per Mille Visitors (Ezoic's metric, roughly comparable but includes multi-page sessions). All figures assume US-majority traffic unless noted.

## Ad Network Comparison

### Entry Requirements (as of early 2026)

| Network | Minimum Traffic | Other Requirements | Typical RPM Range |
|---------|----------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| **Raptive** (fka AdThrive) | 25,000 pageviews/mo (lowered from 100K) | 50%+ US/UK/CA/AU/NZ traffic for sites under 100K PV | $35–$57 |
| **Mediavine** | 50,000 sessions/mo OR $5,000 annual ad revenue | Good standing with Google; Mediavine Journey tier at 1,000 sessions | $15–$40+ |
| **Ezoic** | No minimum | Google MCM partner; tiered access levels | $5–$15 |
| **Google AdSense** | No minimum | Google policy compliance | $2–$8 |

### RPM Benchmarks by Network

**Raptive** is currently the highest-paying premium network for established publishers. A 12-month comparative study (one site switching from Mediavine to Raptive in October 2025) showed:
- Mediavine RPMs: $22–$43, average **$35** (Oct 2024 – Oct 2025)
- Raptive RPMs: $47–$57, average **$54** (first 3 months after switch)

This ~54% RPM lift is consistent with Raptive's published claim of "+$20 RPM" versus prior networks. However, sample size is small and may reflect site-specific factors.

**Mediavine** reports typical RPMs of:
- US traffic: $25–$35 starting RPM
- UK traffic: $15–$25 starting RPM
- Mature sites with optimization: $35–$50+

**Ezoic** typical EPMV of $5–$12, with premium tier reaching $15–$20 for high-value niches.

### Network Recommendation for Programmatic SEO

For a new programmatic site targeting 50K+ sessions within 6–12 months:

| Phase | Network | Why |
|-------|---------|-----|
| 0–1K sessions | Ezoic (free tier) or AdSense | No barriers, revenue is negligible anyway |
| 1K–50K sessions | Mediavine Journey | 2–3× AdSense RPMs, low threshold |
| 50K+ sessions | Mediavine or Raptive | Premium RPMs; Raptive if 50%+ tier-1 geo traffic |

## RPM by Niche Vertical

### Tier 1 — High RPM ($25–$50+)

#### Finance / Personal Finance
- **Typical RPM:** $30–$50+ (Mediavine/Raptive), $10–$20 (Ezoic)
- **Why:** Advertiser CPA for financial products (credit cards, loans, investing platforms) ranges $50–$200+. Advertisers pay premium CPMs to reach users with financial intent. Finance consistently tops every RPM ranking.
- **Advertiser verticals bidding:** Credit cards, insurance, investment platforms, tax software, banking
- **Benchmark:** NeworMedia calculator ranks Business & Finance at **$10.02** baseline RPM (highest tier). Premium networks 3–5× this.
- **Caveat:** YMYL content faces severe E-E-A-T scrutiny (see research/05-legal-scan.md). Pure finance pSEO is high-reward but high-risk from an SEO perspective.

#### Health & Fitness
- **Typical RPM:** $20–$35 (Mediavine/Raptive), $8–$15 (Ezoic)
- **Why:** Health advertisers (supplements, fitness equipment, telehealth, insurance) have strong CPA budgets. Health content generates high session depth.
- **Benchmark:** NeworMedia: **$6.80** baseline RPM (second tier)
- **Caveat:** YMYL risk is extreme for medical content. Fitness/wellness (non-medical) is safer and still commands strong RPMs.

### Tier 2 — Moderate-High RPM ($15–$30)

#### Food & Cooking
- **Typical RPM:** $15–$35 (Mediavine/Raptive), $5–$12 (Ezoic)
- **Why:** Recipe sites have excellent session metrics (high time-on-page, multiple pageviews per session). Food advertisers (grocery delivery, kitchen appliances, meal kits) bid competitively. Recipe pages allow aggressive ad density without high bounce rates.
- **Benchmark:** NeworMedia: **$4.76** baseline RPM (lower tier, but premium networks significantly higher)
- **Real-world data:** Midwest Foodie blog earned $530K+ in Raptive ad revenue in 2025. Even with 20% traffic decline, higher RPMs offset the loss.
- **Key insight:** Food is the #1 Mediavine niche by publisher count. High competition but proven economics.

#### Automotive
- **Typical RPM:** $15–$30 (Mediavine/Raptive), $6–$12 (Ezoic)
- **Why:** Automotive advertisers (OEMs, insurance, parts retailers, loan providers) have high customer lifetime value. Auto content has strong commercial intent.
- **Benchmark:** NeworMedia: **$5.44** baseline RPM (mid tier)
- **Trend:** Automotive programmatic CTV spending increased 35–55% in 2024, signaling growing digital budgets that flow downstream to display.

#### Travel / Outdoor
- **Typical RPM:** $15–$35 (Mediavine/Raptive), $8–$15 (Ezoic)
- **Why:** Travel advertisers (airlines, hotels, booking platforms, gear companies) have high transaction values. Travel content has strong seasonal demand patterns.
- **Benchmark:** NeworMedia: **$10.02** baseline RPM (top tier, tied with finance)
- **Note:** Outdoor/hiking is a sub-niche of travel. Expect slightly lower RPMs than core travel ($12–$25) due to less commercial intent, but gear affiliate revenue supplements ad income.

### Tier 3 — Moderate RPM ($10–$20)

#### Pets / Animals
- **Typical RPM:** $10–$25 (Mediavine/Raptive), $5–$10 (Ezoic)
- **Why:** Pet industry spending is $150B+ annually in the US. Advertisers include pet food brands, insurance, supplies, vet telehealth. Emotional content drives engagement.
- **Benchmark:** NeworMedia: **$6.12** baseline RPM (mid tier)
- **Real-world data:** A pet blog on Mediavine earned $8,453 in 2024 (~$700/mo), suggesting relatively modest RPMs at lower traffic volumes. At scale, pet sites report $15–$25 RPMs.
- **Key insight:** Pet niche is growing fast but RPMs lag finance/food. Strong for programmatic due to structured breed/product data.

#### Parenting / Family
- **Typical RPM:** $10–$25 (Mediavine/Raptive), $5–$10 (Ezoic)
- **Why:** Parenting advertisers (baby products, family insurance, educational toys, family vehicles) are consistent spenders. Content is evergreen and high-engagement.
- **Benchmark:** NeworMedia: **$6.12** baseline RPM (mid tier)
- **Note:** Parenting RPMs are stable year-round with less Q1 dip than other niches due to consistent baby product advertising.

#### Education
- **Typical RPM:** $8–$20 (Mediavine/Raptive), $5–$10 (Ezoic)
- **Why:** Education advertisers (online courses, EdTech platforms, textbooks, test prep) have moderate CPAs. Audience skews younger with less purchasing power.
- **Benchmark:** NeworMedia: **$4.76** baseline RPM (lower tier)
- **Note:** Education RPMs spike during back-to-school (Aug–Sep) and college admissions (Jan–Mar) seasons.

## Seasonal RPM Patterns

### The Annual Cycle

```
RPM Index (100 = yearly average)

Q1          Q2          Q3          Q4
Jan Feb Mar Apr May Jun Jul Aug Sep Oct Nov Dec
 60  65  75  85  95 100  90  95 100 110 130 170
 ▼▼  ▼   ─   ▲   ▲  ▲▲  ▼   ▲  ▲▲  ▲▲ ▲▲▲ ████
```

### Quarter-by-Quarter Breakdown

**Q1 (January–March): The Trough**
- January sees **30–40% RPM drop** from December peaks
- Advertisers reset annual budgets; Q4 spending hangover
- CPMs hit yearly lows in mid-January
- Gradual recovery through February–March
- **Planning implication:** Launch new sites in Q1 when competition for rankings is lower; don't judge monetization by Q1 numbers

**Q2 (April–June): Recovery**
- CPC rates increase **~55% from Q1 to Q2** (2024 data)
- Spring advertising picks up: Mother's Day, graduations, wedding season
- June approaches yearly average RPMs
- **Planning implication:** Good benchmark quarter for "normal" RPMs

**Q3 (July–September): Summer Dip then Recovery**
- July sees the "summer slump" — users outdoors, screen time drops
- August: back-to-school drives education and family advertiser spending
- September: recovery accelerates as Q4 planning begins
- **Planning implication:** Education niches benefit from Aug–Sep spike; most others see a dip

**Q4 (October–December): The Goldmine**
- RPMs increase **70–100%** from start to end of quarter
- Black Friday/Cyber Monday weekend: **200–300% CPM spike** vs. typical October rates
- Desktop CPMs increase ~40% from mid-November to mid-December alone
- Advertisers race to spend remaining annual budgets
- **Planning implication:** Q4 revenue often equals the other three quarters combined. Site must be indexed and ranking by September to capture Q4.

## Key Takeaways for Niche Selection

### 1. RPM Alone Doesn't Determine Winner

A niche with $40 RPM but impossible SEO competition yields $0. A niche with $15 RPM and 100K easy monthly pageviews yields $1,500/month. **Volume × RPM = Revenue.** Programmatic SEO wins on volume.

### 2. Premium Network Access is the Multiplier

The jump from Ezoic ($5–$15 EPMV) to Mediavine/Raptive ($25–$50+ RPM) is a **3–5× revenue multiplier** on the same traffic. Reaching 50K sessions fast should be a primary goal.

### 3. Niche RPM Rankings for Our Candidates

**Best RPM potential (if SEO/legal barriers cleared):**
1. Product alternatives — high commercial intent, strong affiliate + display combo
2. Job salary by city — finance-adjacent, strong advertiser demand
3. City cost of living — real estate/moving advertisers pay premium
4. Car comparisons — deep auto advertiser budgets
5. Food nutrition / Recipe nutrition — proven food blog economics

**Worst RPM potential:**
1. Baby name meanings — low commercial intent, limited advertiser verticals
2. Plant growing by zone — niche gardening advertisers, small budgets
3. College comparisons — moderate EdTech spend but seasonal

### 4. Seasonal Resilience Matters

Niches with stable year-round demand (pets, parenting, food) provide predictable revenue. Seasonal niches (outdoor, education) create cash flow volatility. For a bootstrapped operation, **stable RPM × stable traffic > high peak RPM with deep troughs**.

### 5. The Q4 Imperative

Any site launched for programmatic SEO should target indexing and initial rankings by **September** to capture the Q4 advertising bonanza. Missing Q4 in year one means leaving 30–40% of potential first-year revenue on the table.
